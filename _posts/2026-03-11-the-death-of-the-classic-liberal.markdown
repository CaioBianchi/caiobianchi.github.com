I used to call myself a classic liberal. I believed in the fundamental pillars of individual liberty, free speech, free markets, and a government that existed to serve its people, not to rule over them. But over the last decade, I have watched the political left abandon these principles, plunging headfirst into a brand of creeping authoritarianism that has left me, and millions of other Canadians, politically homeless. Or at least, it did until I realized the only rational choice left was to become a conservative.

We have now watched the Liberal Party govern Canada for 11 consecutive years. We endured the long, divisive Trudeau era, and now we find ourselves under the supposedly "refreshed" Carney government. But let's not kid ourselves: the paint job may be new, but the engine is the same, and it is driving this country off a cliff.

### The Illusion of Change and the Media Machine

Nothing has been more disheartening than watching the Liberals recover from a 20-point deficit in the polls to surge to an election victory, simply by switching out their leader for a central banker. Mark Carney stepped in, and suddenly, the political sins of the past decade were wiped clean. The establishment completely ignored the piling domestic issues and the freefalling standard of living we have endured. 

How did this happen? It happened because of a mainstream media apparatus that has actively manipulated voters. Propped up by massive government subsidies, the media's liberal bias is no longer a subtle undertone; it is a blaring siren. Instead of holding the government accountable for the housing crisis, the inflation crisis, or the ArriveCan and WE Charity scandals, they chose to act as the PR wing of the Liberal Party.

This bias was never more obvious than when we witnessed the largest number of floor-crossers in Canadian political history. We watched elected officials betray their constituents for dark, backroom deals to secure their own power within the Liberal ranks. And the media? They barely covered it. They were too busy nitpicking and aggressively criticizing Pierre Poilievre's leadership to report on the blatant corruption unfolding right in front of us.

### The Carbon Tax Shell Game

Consider the highly publicized "downfall" of the Trudeau-era consumer carbon tax. It was heralded as a massive concession, a sign that the government was finally listening to struggling Canadians. But it was a bait-and-switch. 

The Carney Liberals quietly kept the industrial carbon tax, expanding it and hiding it from the public eye. They know full well that taxing the supply chain inevitably passes the costs directly down to the consumer at the checkout line. It's the exact same financial burden, just hidden behind a curtain of corporate jargon. They didn't axe the tax; they just made it invisible.

### A Government of Distraction and Authoritarianism

Meanwhile, the palpable sense of hopelessness across this country is suffocating. I speak to Canadians every day who have lost their jobs, whose salaries have been effectively halved by inflation and taxation, and who are struggling to keep a roof over their heads. They watch in despair as unprecedented numbers of refugees are housed in hotels and receive better government benefits than the taxpaying citizens who were born here or immigrated through the proper, rigorous channels.

And what is the government's response to this domestic despair? Fear-mongering. They constantly point south, endlessly obsessing over the Trump administration to terrify voters into compliance. It is a cynical, exhausting distraction tactic. Look at the scary man down south, so you don't notice the government freezing the bank accounts of working-class protesters right here at home. 

### The War on Free Speech and Religion

If the economic destruction wasn't enough, the government's aggressive push to regulate what we can say and believe should terrify every Canadian. We are witnessing an unprecedented assault on free expression. Bills like C-11 and the Online Harms Act already set a dangerous precedent, but the recently created Bill C-9 and similar legislation take it a step further. 

These bills look to curb speech under the guise of safety and are now creeping into the territory of regulating religious beliefs and compelling speech. When the state gets to decide what constitutes acceptable speech or what religious convictions are permissible in the public square, we are no longer living in a free society. It is a direct attack on the classic liberal values I once held dear, engineered to silence dissent and punish anyone who steps out of line with the government's approved narrative.

### The One-Party State

I am tired. I am incredibly hopeless about the future of the Canadian economy and our democracy moving forward. When a government can oversee the worst decline in standard of living in modern history, divide its citizens so ruthlessly, engage in endless ethical scandals, and still manipulate its way to an election victory through media subsidies and backroom floor-crossing deals... what democracy is left?

What breaks my heart the most is looking at the current polls. Despite everything this government has done to erode our liberties and our prosperity, the polls are still favourable for the Liberals. I am left in utter disbelief at my fellow Canadians. How can so many people look at the wreckage of the last 11 years and ask for more of the same?

It feels as though Canada is slowly, quietly sliding into a one-party state, if it hasn't become one already. A state where the establishment always wins, where the media protects the ruling class, and where the classic liberal values of freedom and accountability are treated as radical, dangerous ideas. 

I didn't leave the liberal ideology; the left left me. And as I look at the state of this country, I know I am not the only one who has been forced to walk away.
